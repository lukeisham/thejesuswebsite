§ 9 — Essay Content Elements

Essay: The Sermon on the Mount

The Sermon on the Mount represents the most concentrated body of ethical teaching attributed to Jesus of Nazareth. Delivered on a hillside in Galilee, it constitutes the opening of the Matthean discourse complex, spanning chapters five through seven of the first gospel.This is the largest single body of dominical discourse preserved in the Synoptic tradition, though Lukan parallels exist in a "Sermon on the Plain" (Lk 6:17–49).

The Beatitudes, which open the sermon, employ a formulaic blessing structure — Μακάριοι οἱ πτωχοὶ τῷ πνεύματι — that reverses conventional social expectation. The term makarios is consistently rendered "blessed" in the English tradition, though "flourishing" or "deeply fortunate" may better capture the Hebrew cognate ashrei.

Matt. 5:3 — "Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." (ESV)

Subsequent scholarship has debated whether the sermon represents a unified address or a Matthean redactional collection of dispersed logia. The "antitheses" of 5:21–48 ("You have heard it said… but I say to you") suggest a deliberate compositional structure.Cf. Stanton, G. (1992). A Gospel for a New People, p. 288.

France, R.T. The Gospel of Matthew. NICNT. Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 2007. pp. 156–163.
