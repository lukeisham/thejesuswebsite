Books

Jesus and the Eyewitnesses: The Gospels as Eyewitness Testimony

by Richard Bauckham (2006) — Argues convincingly that the Gospels draw from named eyewitness sources and oral tradition preserved with care—countering skeptical “form criticism.”

Fabricating Jesus: How Modern Scholars Distort the Gospels

by Craig A Evans (2007) — An accessible evangelical critique of the Jesus Seminar and revisionist theories (e.g., Gnostic gospels, late dating etc).

The Case for Christ

Lee Strobel (1998) — A journalistic investigation of the evidence for Jesus.

The Resurrection of the Son of God

by N.T. Wright (2003) — A massive, historically grounded case that the bodily resurrection best explains the rise of early Christianity. Wright situates Jesus firmly in first-century Judaism while defending the event as history, not myth.
