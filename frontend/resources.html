Books

Jesus and the Eyewitnesses: The Gospels as eyewitness testimony (ISBN: 9780802831620)

by Richard Bauckham (2006) — Argues convincingly that the Gospels draw from named eyewitness sources and oral tradition preserved with care—countering skeptical “form criticism.”

Fabricating Jesus: How Modern Scholars Distort the Gospels (ISBN: 9780830833559)

by Craig A Evans (2007) — An accessible evangelical critique of the Jesus Seminar and revisionist theories (e.g., Gnostic gospels, late dating etc).

Jesus and the Rise of Early Christianity (ISBN: 9780830826995)

by Paul Barnett (1999) — Barnett’s primary thesis is that Christianity is an "historical" religion, he is also a good example of the 'logic of history' approach.

Undesigned Coincidences (ISBN: 9781314776430)

by Lydia McGrew (2017) — An undesigned coincidence is an apparently casual, yet puzzle-like "fit" between two or more texts, and its best explanation is that the authors knew the truth about the events they describe or allude to.
