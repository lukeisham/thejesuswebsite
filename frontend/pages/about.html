About the Jesus Website

Who I am, my methodology, a brief summary of the tech stack and the opportunity for you to contribute.

Updated: April 2026

I'm the pastor of Balaclava Presbyterian Church, and created this website as a ministry side project. I wanted to see how vibe-coding worked and provide a useful resource for thinking about the historical evidence for Jesus. That is helpful for humans and accessible for AI agents.

Methodology

When thinking about the history of Jesus, I think a 'logic of history' approach is best. For me that means that individuals interact with an independent objective reality and can determine what occured in the past by making logical inferences based on what can be observed in available information, or verified already.

AI agents have been used to build code, find evidence, verify sources and organise the records. Wherever possible I have included references to both the primary and secondary sources, allowing you the reader to verify the evidence for yourself or make it easy for an AI agent to verify the veracity of the information. You will also notice that I've used AI-generated reconstructions and illustrations. These are purely rehotical aides to help you understand and imagine what the evidence is describing. The distinction between illustrations and photographs is strictly adhered to throughout the website.

Tech Stack

The Jesus Website is built as a Multi-Page Application (MPA) using a minimalist, archival-style design system. I used vanilla HTML, CSS, JavaScript, SQLite (WASM) and Python to build the website. My vibe-coding was built around markdown guides that controlled the code design, creation, debugging and revision. You can view Github respository here .
