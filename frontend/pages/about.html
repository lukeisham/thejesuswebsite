About the Jesus Website

An archive dedicated to the historical evidence of Jesus of Nazareth.

Updated: April 2026

Welcome to The Jesus Website. Our mission is to provide a comprehensive, evidence-based archive of historical data, archaeological findings, and scholarly analysis regarding the life, teachings, and death of Jesus of Nazareth. We aim to present this information with the weight of a physical museum and the precision of a modern digital repository.

Our Methodology

We follow a strict historiographical methodology, prioritizing primary sources, archaeological evidence, and critical academic research. Every record in our database is cross-referenced with multiple independent witnesses, following the standards of historical inquiry used by museums and international archives.

Our data is organized into a relational structure that highlights the connections between different pieces of evidence, from biblical manuscripts to non-Christian historical mentions and physical artifacts.

Technical Architecture

The Jesus Website is built as a high-performance Multi-Page Application (MPA) using a minimalist, archival-first design system. We utilize:

  • SQLite (WASM): A client-side database engine that allows for rapid, secure querying of historical records without the need for a heavy server-side backend.
  • Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS: A commitment to speed and long-term maintainability, avoiding the bloat of modern frameworks.
  • Multi-Agent Ready: An architecture designed to be easily read and understood by both humans and AI agents, ensuring the archive remains accessible in the age of generative search.

Contact & Contribution

We welcome scholarly input and correction. If you identify a discrepancy or have evidence to contribute, please reach out via our primary archival portal or visit our GitHub repository to review our documentation and source data.
