List of Historical Sources

The primary written sources for the historical Jesus — from the canonical Gospels and Pauline letters to non-Christian historians and early church fathers.

Canonical Gospels

  • Mark c. AD 65–70 — The earliest Gospel; fast-paced account likely drawing on Petrine testimony.
  • Matthew c. AD 80–90 — Jewish-focused Gospel emphasising Jesus as the fulfilment of OT scripture.
  • Luke c. AD 80–90 — Addressed to Theophilus; written by a companion of Paul; includes the birth narratives.
  • John c. AD 90–100 — Theological and distinctive in content; "I Am" sayings; signs; Farewell Discourse.

Pauline Epistles

  • 1 Thessalonians c. AD 49–51 — Earliest Paul; references Jesus' death, resurrection and return.
  • Galatians c. AD 48–55 — Paul met James (Jesus' brother) and Peter in Jerusalem; early creedal material.
  • 1 Corinthians c. AD 53–54 — Contains the earliest written resurrection creed (15:3–8) and the Last Supper account (11:23–26).
  • 2 Corinthians c. AD 55–56 — Paul defends his apostleship; references the earthly life and death of Jesus.
  • Romans c. AD 57 — Paul's most systematic theological letter; Jesus as the second Adam.
  • Philippians c. AD 60–62 — Contains the Christ-hymn (2:6–11): pre-existence, incarnation, death, exaltation.
  • Philemon c. AD 60–62 — Personal letter; evidence of Paul's imprisonment and social context.

Other New Testament Sources

  • Acts of the Apostles c. AD 80–90 — Luke's sequel; early speeches summarise the gospel of Jesus' death and resurrection.
  • Hebrews c. AD 60–90 — Argues for Jesus as the superior high priest; references his suffering and earthly life.
  • James c. AD 48–62 — Written by Jesus' brother; echoes of the Sermon on the Mount throughout.
  • 1 Peter c. AD 64–68 — Attributed to the apostle Peter; references the suffering, death and resurrection of Jesus.

Non-Christian Sources

  • Josephus — Antiquities of the Jews c. AD 93–94 — Two references: the Testimonium Flavianum (18.3.3) and the execution of James "the brother of Jesus" (20.9.1).
  • Tacitus — Annals c. AD 116 — Records that "Christus" was executed under Pontius Pilate during Tiberius' reign; Christians blamed for the fire of Rome.
  • Pliny the Younger — Letters c. AD 112 — Describes Christians in Bithynia who sing hymns to Christ "as to a god."
  • Suetonius — Lives of the Twelve Caesars c. AD 121 — References Jews expelled from Rome due to disturbances over "Chrestus."
  • Babylonian Talmud Sanhedrin 43a — Hostile reference to Jesus being hanged on Passover Eve for leading Israel astray.
  • Lucian of Samosata — The Death of Peregrinus c. AD 170 — Satirical account that references Jesus as the "crucified sophist" worshipped by Christians.

Early Church Fathers

  • Clement of Rome — 1 Clement c. AD 96 — Cites Jesus' teaching; evidence of apostolic tradition in the early church.
  • Ignatius of Antioch — Letters c. AD 108–117 — Seven letters affirming Jesus' birth of Mary, death under Pilate, and resurrection.
  • Polycarp of Smyrna — Philippians c. AD 110 — Letter with extensive quotation of Jesus' sayings; martyred c. AD 155.
  • Papias — Expositions of the Lord's Oracles c. AD 130 — Reports that Mark wrote Peter's teachings; Matthew composed sayings of the Lord.
  • Justin Martyr — Apologies c. AD 150–160 — Defends Christianity to the emperor; describes Christian worship and Jesus' teaching.
