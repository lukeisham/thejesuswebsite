Events in and around the Roman Empire (0 – 100 AD)

The first century was defined by the transition from Republic to Empire. These secular events provided the infrastructure, legal landscape, and political tensions in which the early Christian movement began.

0 – 100 AD (Key Imperial & Historical Events)

    0 – 100 AD (Global Historical Events)

    • Reign of Augustus 27 BC – 14 AD — The first Emperor. He established the *Pax Romana*, a period of relative peace and stability that facilitated travel across the Mediterranean.
    • Restoration of the Han Dynasty 25 AD — After the brief and chaotic Xin Dynasty (9–23 AD), Emperor Guangwu restored the "Eastern Han." This era saw the invention of paper and the first official introduction of Buddhism to China via the Silk Road.
    • Battle of the Teutoburg Forest 9 AD — A massive Roman defeat by Germanic tribes that ended Roman hopes of expanding to the Elbe River, fixing the border at the Rhine.
    • Reign of Tiberius 14 – 37 AD — Successor to Augustus. It was during his reign that Jesus conducted his ministry and was executed in Judea.
    • Kandake Amanitara of Meroë c. 20 – 40 AD — Queen of Kush (modern Sudan). The conversion of her treasurer (the Ethiopian Eunuch in Acts 8) represents the earliest biblical record of the Gospel reaching Sub-Saharan Africa.
    • Rise of the Kushan Empire c. 30 – 80 AD — Kujula Kadphises united the Yuezhi confederation in Northern India/Afghanistan, creating a critical hub for trade between Rome and China.
    • Reign of Caligula 37 – 41 AD — Known for extreme autocracy; his plan to desecrate the Temple in Jerusalem nearly sparked a Jewish revolt decades early.
    • Annexation of Mauretania 40 – 44 AD — Following the execution of Ptolemy of Mauretania by Caligula, Claudius officially annexed the region (modern Morocco/Algeria) into the Empire.
    • Conquest of Britain 43 AD — Emperor Claudius successfully invaded Britain, adding a major new province to the Roman Empire.
    • The Periplus of the Erythraean Sea c. 40 – 70 AD — A Greco-Roman maritime log describing trade routes from Egypt to India, confirming the massive scale of 1st-century Roman-Indian trade.
    • Roman–Parthian War over Armenia 58 – 63 AD — A major conflict between Rome and Persia (Parthia). It ended in a diplomatic compromise regarding the sovereignty of the Armenian buffer state.
    • The Great Fire of Rome 64 AD — Destroyed much of the capital. Nero used the local Christian community as a scapegoat, initiating the first localized Roman persecution.
    • First Jewish-Roman War 66 – 73 AD — A widespread revolt in Judea that led to a massive Roman military response and internal political upheaval.
    • Year of the Four Emperors 69 AD — Civil war following Nero’s death. Vespasian emerged victorious, beginning the Flavian Dynasty.
    • Destruction of the Second Temple 70 AD — Titus, son of Vespasian, breached Jerusalem and destroyed the Temple, a turning point for both Judaism and the early Church.
    • Reign of Pacorus II 78 – 110 AD — A period of infrastructure growth in the Parthian Empire (Persia), maintaining the Silk Road through which Eastern Christian missions traveled.
    • Eruption of Mount Vesuvius 79 AD — Destroyed Pompeii and Herculaneum; the resulting archaeological record provides the clearest view of 1st-century Roman life.
    • Construction of the Colosseum 80 AD — The Flavian Amphitheatre was dedicated, serving as the premier venue for Roman public spectacles and executions.
    • Reign of Domitian 81 – 96 AD — A period of increased imperial cult worship and tension with the Senate, ending with his assassination.
    • Accession of Trajan 98 AD — The beginning of the "Five Good Emperors" era, during which the Empire reached its maximum territorial size.
    • Rise of Teotihuacán c. 1 – 100 AD — In the Valley of Mexico, Teotihuacán shifted from a collection of villages into a planned urban metropolis. Construction began on the massive Pyramid of the Sun, establishing the city as the dominant religious and economic power in Mesoamerica.
