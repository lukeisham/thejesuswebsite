List of Significant Objects

Artefacts, tokens and symbolic objects connected to the life, death and tradition of Jesus — from the instruments of the Passion to key archaeological discoveries.

Passion Objects

  • The Cross — The instrument of crucifixion; central symbol of Christian faith and the means of Jesus' death.
  • Crown of Thorns Matthew 27:29 — Twisted and placed on Jesus' head in mockery of his kingly claims.
  • The Spear (Holy Lance) John 19:34 — Used by a Roman soldier to pierce Jesus' side, resulting in a flow of blood and water.
  • The Titulus (Inscription) John 19:19–22 — The sign placed above Jesus' head reading "Jesus of Nazareth, King of the Jews" in three languages.
  • Nails John 20:25 — Thomas refers to the nail marks as proof of identity when he meets the risen Jesus.
  • Reed & Sponge Mark 15:36 — Wine vinegar offered to Jesus on a reed as he hung on the cross.
  • The Purple Robe John 19:2 — Placed on Jesus by soldiers as a costume of royal mockery during his trial.
  • Seamless Robe John 19:23–24 — Soldiers cast lots rather than divide it; fulfilling Psalm 22:18.
  • Burial Shroud Mark 15:46 — The linen cloth purchased by Joseph of Arimathea to wrap Jesus' body.

Liturgical Tokens

  • Bread & Wine Mark 14:22–25 — Instituted at the Last Supper as the Eucharist; the new covenant in his body and blood.
  • Baptismal Water Mark 1:9 — Water of the Jordan River used in Jesus' baptism; the sacrament of initiation and rebirth.
  • Anointing Oil Mark 14:3 — Expensive nard poured on Jesus at Bethany, anticipating and preparing his body for burial.
  • Basin & Towel John 13:4–5 — The tools Jesus used to wash the disciples' feet, symbolizing the "Mandatum" of servanthood.

Symbolic & Ministry Objects

  • Thirty Silver Coins Matthew 26:15 — The price of betrayal; later returned by Judas and used to purchase the "Field of Blood."
  • Fisher's Net Mark 1:17 — A tool of the trade that became a metaphor for the mission of the Apostles.
  • Mustard Seed Mark 4:31 — Used by Jesus as a physical object lesson for the organic, expansive nature of faith.
  • Alabaster Jar Mark 14:3 — Broken to release its contents; symbolizing the total and costly devotion of the worshipper.
  • The Shepherd’s Staff John 10:11 — Evoked in Jesus' "I Am" discourse to represent guidance, protection, and authority.
  • The Scourge (Whip) John 2:15 — Hand-made by Jesus from cords to drive the money-changers from the Temple.
