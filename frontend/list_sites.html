List of Archaeological Sites

Archaeological sites in Israel and surrounding regions related to the life of Jesus, the Gospels, and the people mentioned in the records.

Current & Active Archaeological Sites

  • Magdala (Taricheae) — IAA Site ID: 1042/0 — Significant excavations revealing a first-century synagogue and town on the Sea of Galilee. Lead: Universidad Anáhuac (Mexico) & Israel Antiquities Authority.
  • Pool of Siloam — IAA Site ID: 3000/0 — Massive active excavation to uncover the full perimeter of the Second Temple-period pool. Lead: Israel Antiquities Authority & City of David Foundation.
  • Capernaum (Kfar Nahum) — IAA Site ID: 1025/0 — Ongoing study and conservation of the 4th-century white synagogue and Peter’s House. Lead: Studium Biblicum Franciscanum.
  • Caesarea Maritima — IAA Site ID: 1004/0 — Multi-year restoration and excavation of the Roman vaults and Herodian harbor. Lead: New York University & Tel Aviv University.
  • Sepphoris (Zippori) — IAA Site ID: 1085/0 — Study of the "Mona Lisa of the Galilee" mosaics and Roman street grid. Lead: Hebrew University of Jerusalem.
  • Mount Zion (Zion Slopes) — IAA Site ID: 3001/0 — Exploration of the Iron Age and Byzantine-era city walls and priestly mansions. Lead: University of North Carolina at Charlotte.
  • Tel Dan — IAA Site ID: 1001/0 — Ongoing study of the "House of David" inscription and the Iron Age gate complex. Lead: Nelson Glueck School of Biblical Archaeology (HUC-JIR).
  • Hazor (Tel Hazor) — IAA Site ID: 1052/0 — Excavation of the largest Canaanite and Israelite city in the north. Lead: The Selz Foundation Hazor Excavations (Hebrew University).
  • Bethsaida (et-Tell / el-Araj) — IAA Site ID: 1032/0 — Competing excavations seeking to definitively identify the hometown of Peter and Andrew. Lead: University of Nebraska Omaha / Kinneret College.

Former & Historically Significant Sites

  • Pools of Bethesda — Survey ID: 3005/0 — Confirmed the complex archaeological structure described in the Gospel of John. Lead Group (Historic): Missionaries of Africa (White Fathers).
  • Masada — UNESCO No: 1040 — Major 1960s excavations; currently a preserved National Park and World Heritage site. Lead Group (Historic): Israel Exploration Society & Yigael Yadin.
  • The Garden Tomb — Historic Survey — A Protestant alternative to the Holy Sepulchre; primarily maintained for education and worship. Lead: The Garden Tomb (Jerusalem) Association.
  • Church of the Holy Sepulchre — Active Conservation — Site of the recent "Edicule" restoration (2016) and structural analysis. Lead: National Technical University of Athens (Scientific Team).
  • Herodium (Jebel Fureidis) — IAA Site ID: 2045/0 — Site of Herod the Great's tomb discovery and massive palace architecture. Lead: The Ehud Netzer Expedition (Hebrew University).
