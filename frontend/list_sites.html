List of Archaeological Sites

Archaeological sites in Israel and surrounding regions related to the life of Jesus, the Gospels, and the people mentioned in the records.

Current & Active Archaeological Sites

  • Magdala — Significant excavations reveling a first-century synagogue and town on the Sea of Galilee.
  • Pool of Siloam — Recent excavations in Jerusalem continue to uncover the full extent of this Gospel-related pool.
  • Capernaum (Synagogue & House of Peter) — Highly active destination; ongoing preservation and study of the basalt ruins.
  • Caesarea Maritima — Massive site on the coast; active excavations of the Roman theatre, palace, and harbour.
  • Sepphoris (Zippori) — Extensive excavations of the Roman-era city near Nazareth.
  • Mount Zion/Caiaphas House — Continued exploration of the Herodian-era remains on the southern slope.

Former & Historically Significant Sites

  • Pools of Bethesda — Early 20th-century excavations that confirmed the five-porch structure described in John.
  • Masada — Major excavations in the 1960s; now primarily a preserved historic monument.
  • The Garden Tomb — Identified in the late 19th century as a potential site for the crucifixion and burial.
  • Church of the Holy Sepulchre — Built over the traditional site identified in the 4th century; site of countless historical surveys.
  • Herodium — The palace-fortress of Herod the Great, excavated extensively in the 20th century.
