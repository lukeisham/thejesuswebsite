Old Testament Verses Cited

Old Testament passages quoted by Jesus, applied to him by New Testament authors, or understood as messianic prophecies fulfilled in his life and death.

Messianic Prophecies

  • Isaiah 7:14 — "A virgin will conceive and give birth to a son" — applied to the nativity (Mt 1:23).
  • Isaiah 9:6 — "A child will be born to us… Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace."
  • Isaiah 53 — The Suffering Servant passage; foundational to the understanding of Jesus' atoning death.
  • Micah 5:2 — "But you, Bethlehem… out of you will come a ruler over Israel" (Mt 2:6).
  • Zechariah 9:9 — "See, your king comes to you… riding on a donkey" — the Triumphal Entry (Mt 21:5).
  • Psalm 110:1 — "The Lord said to my Lord: Sit at my right hand" — cited by Jesus in debate (Mk 12:36).

Cited by Jesus

  • Deuteronomy 6:5 — "Love the Lord your God with all your heart" — the Greatest Commandment (Mk 12:30).
  • Leviticus 19:18 — "Love your neighbour as yourself" — the second commandment (Mk 12:31).
  • Psalm 22:1 — "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?" — Jesus' cry from the cross (Mk 15:34).
  • Isaiah 61:1–2 — "The Spirit of the Lord is on me" — Jesus reads this in the Nazareth synagogue (Lk 4:18–19).
  • Hosea 6:6 — "I desire mercy, not sacrifice" — cited twice by Jesus to the Pharisees (Mt 9:13; 12:7).

Passion Fulfilments

  • Psalm 22 — Details of mocking, piercing, and the casting of lots for garments all appear in the crucifixion accounts.
  • Psalm 69:21 — "They gave me vinegar for my thirst" — the sponge of wine vinegar (Mk 15:36).
  • Zechariah 11:12–13 — Thirty pieces of silver thrown into the house of the Lord — applied to Judas' betrayal money (Mt 27:9–10).
  • Isaiah 50:6 — "I offered my back to those who beat me… did not hide my face from mocking and spitting."
  • Exodus 12:46 — "Do not break any of the bones" — the Passover lamb ruling; applied to Jesus (Jn 19:36).

Applied by NT Authors

  • Isaiah 40:3 — "A voice calling in the wilderness" — applied to John the Baptist (Mk 1:3).
  • Malachi 3:1 — "I will send my messenger ahead of you" — applied to John the Baptist (Mk 1:2).
  • Psalm 118:22 — "The stone the builders rejected has become the cornerstone" — applied to Jesus (Mk 12:10; Acts 4:11).
  • Jeremiah 31:15 — "Rachel weeping for her children" — applied to the massacre of the innocents (Mt 2:18).
  • Daniel 7:13–14 — "One like a son of man… coming with the clouds" — Jesus' self-designation and eschatological reference (Mk 13:26).
