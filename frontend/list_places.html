List of Places

The geographic locations associated with the life and ministry of Jesus, from Galilee to Jerusalem and the wider Roman world.

Galilee

  • Nazareth — Hometown of Jesus; where he grew up and was initially rejected (Lk 4:16–30).
  • Capernaum — Base of Jesus' Galilean ministry; site of many healings and the synagogue teaching.
  • Cana — Site of the first sign: water turned to wine at a wedding (Jn 2:1–11).
  • Sea of Galilee — Where Jesus called his first disciples, calmed the storm, and walked on water.
  • Mount Tabor — Traditional site of the Transfiguration.
  • Bethsaida — Hometown of Philip, Peter and Andrew; site of the healing of a blind man (Mk 8:22).

Jerusalem

  • Temple Mount — Centre of Jewish worship; cleansed by Jesus; the focus of the Olivet Discourse.
  • Golgotha ("Place of the Skull") — Site of the crucifixion, just outside the city walls.
  • Garden Tomb — Traditional site of the burial and resurrection of Jesus.
  • Gethsemane — Garden on the Mount of Olives where Jesus prayed before his arrest.
  • The Upper Room — Location of the Last Supper and the post-resurrection appearances.
  • Pool of Bethesda — Site of the healing of the paralysed man who had been ill 38 years (Jn 5).
  • Pool of Siloam — Where Jesus sent the man born blind to wash mud from his eyes (Jn 9).

Judea & Perea

  • Bethlehem — Birthplace of Jesus; the city of David (Lk 2:4–7).
  • Jericho — Where Bartimaeus received his sight and Zacchaeus repented.
  • Jordan River — Site of Jesus' baptism by John (Mk 1:9).
  • Bethany — Home of Mary, Martha and Lazarus; site of the anointing and the raising of Lazarus.
  • Judean Wilderness — Where Jesus fasted 40 days and was tempted by Satan.
  • Qumran — Home of the Essene community and the Dead Sea Scrolls; near Jesus' baptism site.

Wider Levant

  • Tyre & Sidon — Coastal Phoenician cities; Jesus healed the Syrophoenician woman's daughter nearby.
  • Caesarea Philippi — Site of Peter's confession: "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" (Mt 16:16).
  • Samaria / Sychar — Where Jesus spoke to the Samaritan woman at the well (Jn 4).
  • Damascus — Paul encountered the risen Jesus on the road to this city (Acts 9).

Rome & the Empire

  • Rome — Imperial capital; Tacitus records the execution of Christians under Nero; Paul writes to the Roman church.
  • Caesarea Maritima — Roman administrative capital of Judea; where Pilate likely resided.
  • Antioch — First city where followers of Jesus were called "Christians" (Acts 11:26).
