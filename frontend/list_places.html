List of Places

The geographic locations associated with the life and ministry of Jesus, from Galilee to Jerusalem and the wider Roman world.

Galilee

  • Nazareth (Luke 2:39–40) — Hometown of Jesus where he grew up and was later rejected by his own people (Lk 4:16–30).
  • Capernaum (Matthew 4:13) — The headquarters of Jesus' Galilean ministry and home to Peter; site of the healing of the Centurion's servant.
  • Cana (John 2:11) — The village where Jesus performed his first sign by turning water into wine at a wedding feast (Jn 2).
  • Sea of Galilee (Lake Gennesaret) (Mark 1:16–20) — The setting for the calling of the fishermen, the calming of the storm, and the walking on water.
  • Bethsaida (John 1:44) — Hometown of Philip, Peter, and Andrew; site of the feeding of the five thousand (Lk 9:10).
  • Magdala (Matthew 15:39) — The home of Mary Magdalene and a major fishing hub on the western shore.
  • Nain (Luke 7:11) — A village where Jesus raised a widow’s only son from the dead (Lk 7:11-17).
  • Mount of Transfiguration (Matthew 17:1–2) — Traditionally identified as Mount Tabor or Mount Hermon; where Jesus’ glory was revealed to Peter, James, and John.

Jerusalem

  • The Temple (Matthew 21:12–14) — The center of Jewish religious life; the site of Jesus' presentation as a child and his later cleansing of the courts.
  • Golgotha (John 19:17–18) — The "Place of the Skull" outside the city walls where the crucifixion took place.
  • Gethsemane (Matthew 26:36) — A garden at the foot of the Mount of Olives where Jesus endured his agony and was betrayed.
  • Mount of Olives (Luke 19:29–37) — The site of the Olivet Discourse and Jesus' triumphal entry; also the location of his Ascension (Lk 24:50).
  • The Upper Room (Cenacle) (Mark 14:13–16) — The traditional site of the Last Supper and where the disciples gathered after the resurrection.
  • Pool of Bethesda (John 5:2–9) — A pool with five porticoes where Jesus healed a man paralyzed for thirty-eight years.
  • Pool of Siloam (John 9:7) — The site where the man born blind washed away the mud to receive his sight (Jn 9).
  • Praetorium (Mark 15:16) — The residence of Pontius Pilate (likely Herod's Palace) where Jesus was tried and sentenced.

Judea & Perea

  • Bethlehem (Matthew 2:1) — The city of David and the birthplace of Jesus, fulfilling Micah's prophecy.
  • Bethany (John 11:1) — Home of Mary, Martha, and Lazarus; located on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives.
  • Jericho (Luke 19:1–2) — An ancient city where Jesus healed blind Bartimaeus and dined with Zacchaeus the tax collector.
  • Jordan River (Matthew 3:13) — The site of Jesus’ baptism by John the Baptist, marking the beginning of his public ministry.
  • Judean Wilderness (Mark 1:12–13) — The desolate region where Jesus fasted for forty days and was tempted by the devil.
  • Emmaus (Luke 24:13) — A village near Jerusalem where the risen Jesus appeared to two disciples on the road (Lk 24).
  • Caesarea Maritima (Acts 10:1) — The Roman capital of the province and home of Pontius Pilate; mentioned implicitly as the seat of Roman authority.
  • Arimathea (Matthew 27:57) — The hometown of Joseph, the wealthy Sanhedrin member who provided the tomb for Jesus.

Samaria & The Wider Region

  • Sychar (John 4:5) — The Samaritan village where Jesus met the woman at Jacob’s Well (Jn 4).
  • Caesarea Philippi (Matthew 16:13) — Located at the base of Mount Hermon; the site of Peter’s confession of Christ’s divinity.
  • Tyre & Sidon (Mark 7:24) — Gentile coastal cities in Phoenicia where Jesus healed the daughter of a Canaanite woman (Mt 15).
  • The Decapolis (Mark 7:31) — A federation of ten Gentile cities where Jesus healed a deaf-mute man and fed the four thousand.
  • Egypt (Matthew 2:13–15) — Where Joseph and Mary fled with the infant Jesus to escape the massacre of the innocents by Herod (Mt 2).
