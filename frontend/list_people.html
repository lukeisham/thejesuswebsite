List of People

The individuals connected to Jesus in the Gospel accounts — his inner circle, family, followers, opponents and the authorities who judged him.

The Twelve Apostles

  • Peter (Simon bar Jonah) — Fisherman from Bethsaida; leader of the Twelve; first to confess Jesus as Messiah.
  • Andrew — Peter's brother; the first disciple called according to John.
  • James (son of Zebedee) — Fisherman; one of the inner three; martyred by Herod Agrippa (Acts 12).
  • John (son of Zebedee) — Brother of James; likely the Beloved Disciple; author of the Johannine corpus.
  • Philip — From Bethsaida; brought Nathanael to Jesus.
  • Bartholomew — Likely identified with Nathanael; called "a true Israelite."
  • Matthew (Levi) — Tax collector; called from his booth at Capernaum.
  • Thomas (Didymus, "the Twin") — Doubted the resurrection until he saw the wounds.
  • James (son of Alphaeus) — Distinguishable from the son of Zebedee; little further detail in the Gospels.
  • Thaddaeus (Judas son of James) — Named in Luke and Acts; asked Jesus why he would reveal himself only to the disciples.
  • Simon the Zealot — Possibly connected to the Zealot political movement.
  • Judas Iscariot — Treasurer of the group; betrayed Jesus for thirty silver coins.

Family

  • Mary — Mother of Jesus; present at the nativity, the wedding at Cana, and the crucifixion.
  • Joseph — Carpenter from Nazareth; legal father of Jesus; last mentioned in the Temple visit (Lk 2).
  • James (brother of Jesus) — Leader of the Jerusalem church; author of the epistle; executed c. AD 62.
  • Jude (brother of Jesus) — Author of the epistle; known from Paul's list of the Lord's brothers (1 Cor 9:5).

Women Disciples

  • Mary Magdalene — Freed from seven demons; present at the crucifixion and the first witness to the resurrection.
  • Martha — Sister of Mary and Lazarus; known for active service and her confession of faith in John 11.
  • Mary of Bethany — Sat at Jesus' feet to learn; anointed him with expensive perfume before his death.
  • Salome — Likely the mother of James and John; present at the crucifixion and empty tomb.
  • Joanna — Wife of Chuza, Herod's household manager; supported Jesus' ministry financially.
  • Susanna — Named alongside Joanna as a supporter of Jesus' ministry (Lk 8:3).

Allies & Followers

  • Nicodemus — Pharisee and member of the Sanhedrin; visited Jesus at night; helped bury him.
  • Joseph of Arimathea — Secret disciple; provided his own tomb for Jesus' burial.
  • Lazarus — Brother of Mary and Martha; raised from the dead four days after burial.
  • Zacchaeus — Chief tax collector in Jericho; climbed a tree to see Jesus; repented and made restitution.
  • Simon of Cyrene — Compelled to carry Jesus' cross on the road to Golgotha.

Opponents & Authorities

  • Caiaphas — High Priest who presided over the Sanhedrin trial; declared Jesus worthy of death.
  • Annas — Former High Priest; father-in-law of Caiaphas; Jesus was brought to him first after arrest.
  • Herod Antipas — Tetrarch of Galilee; executed John the Baptist; mocked Jesus at trial.
  • Pontius Pilate — Roman prefect of Judea; sentenced Jesus to crucifixion despite finding no guilt.
  • Herod the Great — King of Judea at Jesus' birth; ordered the massacre of infants in Bethlehem.

Prophetic Figures

  • John the Baptist — Prophet who prepared the way; baptised Jesus; executed by Herod Antipas.
  • Simeon — Devout man in Jerusalem who recognised the infant Jesus as the Messiah (Lk 2:25–35).
  • Anna — Prophetess in the Temple; recognised Jesus at the Presentation and spoke of him publicly (Lk 2:36–38).
