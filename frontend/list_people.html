List of People

The individuals connected to Jesus in the Gospel accounts — his inner circle, family, followers, opponents and the authorities who judged him.

The Twelve Apostles

  • Peter (Simon bar Jonah) Matthew 16:16-18 — Fisherman from Bethsaida; leader of the Twelve; first to confess Jesus as Messiah.
  • Andrew John 1:40-42 — Peter's brother; the first disciple called according to the Gospel of John.
  • James (son of Zebedee) Mark 3:17 — Fisherman; one of the "Sons of Thunder" and part of the inner circle of three.
  • John (son of Zebedee) John 13:23 — Brother of James; likely the "Beloved Disciple" and author of the Fourth Gospel.
  • Philip John 1:43-46 — From Bethsaida; the one who brought Nathanael to meet Jesus.
  • Bartholomew Matthew 10:3 — Listed in the apostolic groups; often identified with Nathanael.
  • Nathanael John 1:47-49 — Described by Jesus as "a true Israelite in whom there is no deceit."
  • Matthew (Levi) Matthew 9:9 — Tax collector called from his booth at Capernaum to follow Jesus.
  • Thomas (Didymus) John 20:24-28 — Expressed doubt regarding the resurrection until seeing Jesus' wounds firsthand.
  • James (son of Alphaeus) Mark 3:18 — Distinguishable from the son of Zebedee; often referred to as "James the Less."
  • Thaddaeus (Judas son of James) John 14:22 — Mentioned during the Last Supper questioning Jesus about his revelation to the world.
  • Simon the Zealot Luke 6:15 — Distinguished by his title, possibly indicating a former political affiliation.
  • Judas Iscariot Matthew 26:14-16 — The treasurer who betrayed Jesus to the authorities for thirty pieces of silver.

Family

  • Mary Luke 1:26-38 — Mother of Jesus; present at the nativity, the wedding at Cana, and the crucifixion.
  • Joseph Matthew 1:18-25 — Carpenter from Nazareth and legal father of Jesus; last mentioned during the Temple visit.
  • James (brother of Jesus) Matthew 13:55 — Mentioned by the people of Nazareth as one of the brothers of the Lord.
  • Jude (brother of Jesus) Mark 6:3 — Identified among the family members of Jesus in his hometown.

Women Disciples

  • Mary Magdalene Luke 8:2 — Healed of seven demons; a prominent follower and the first witness to the resurrection.
  • Martha Luke 10:38-42 — Sister of Mary and Lazarus; known for her hospitality and her confession of Christ.
  • Mary of Bethany John 12:1-3 — Anointed Jesus’ feet with costly perfume in an act of profound devotion.
  • Salome Mark 15:40 — Present at the crucifixion; likely the mother of the sons of Zebedee.
  • Joanna Luke 8:3 — Wife of Herod’s manager, Chuza; one of the women who supported the ministry financially.
  • Susanna Luke 8:3 — Named as one of the women who traveled with and provided for the disciples.
  • Mary (mother of James and Joseph) Matthew 27:56 — Witness to the crucifixion and the burial of Jesus.

Allies & Followers

  • Nicodemus John 3:1-10 — Pharisee who visited Jesus at night; later defended him and assisted in his burial.
  • Joseph of Arimathea Matthew 27:57-60 — A wealthy member of the Council and secret disciple who provided the tomb.
  • Lazarus John 11:1-44 — Close friend of Jesus whom he raised from the dead in Bethany.
  • Zacchaeus Luke 19:1-10 — Chief tax collector in Jericho whose life was transformed after a personal encounter with Jesus.
  • Simon of Cyrene Mark 15:21 — A passerby compelled by Roman soldiers to carry Jesus' cross to Golgotha.
  • Bartimaeus Mark 10:46-52 — A blind beggar at Jericho who persistently called out to Jesus for healing.

Opponents & Authorities

  • Caiaphas John 11:49-50 — The High Priest who argued it was better for one man to die for the people.
  • Annas John 18:13 — Former High Priest and influential father-in-law of Caiaphas who first questioned Jesus.
  • Herod Antipas Luke 23:7-12 — Tetrarch of Galilee; he questioned and mocked Jesus during the Passion.
  • Pontius Pilate Matthew 27:11-26 — Roman governor who authorized the crucifixion to satisfy the crowd.
  • Herod the Great Matthew 2:1-16 — King of Judea who ordered the massacre of the innocents in Bethlehem.
  • Barabbas Mark 15:6-15 — An insurrectionist released by Pilate in place of Jesus at the crowd's demand.
  • Malchus John 18:10 — The high priest's servant whose ear was cut off by Peter and healed by Jesus.

Prophetic Figures

  • John the Baptist Matthew 3:1-12 — The forerunner of Christ who preached repentance in the wilderness.
  • Simeon Luke 2:25-35 — Recognized the infant Jesus as the "Light for revelation to the Gentiles."
  • Anna Luke 2:36-38 — An aged prophetess who gave thanks to God upon seeing the child Jesus in the Temple.
