List of Manuscripts

Early Christian manuscripts dating from the first five centuries, organised chronologically in 100-year blocks. This list covers key fragements, papyri and major uncial codices.

33 – 200 AD

  • P52 GA: P52 (Rylands Library) — The oldest widely accepted fragment of the New Testament (John 18).
  • P66 GA: P66 (Fondation Bodmer) — A near-complete codex of the Gospel of John.
  • P46 GA: P46 (Chester Beatty Library) — The earliest collection of Pauline epistles.
  • P75 GA: P75 (Fondation Bodmer) — Extensive remains of Luke and John.
  • P104 GA: P104 (Sackler Library) — An exceptionally early fragment of Matthew 21.
  • P90 GA: P90 (Sackler Library) — A small fragment of John 18; a second very early witness to the Fourth Gospel.
  • P64/P67 GA: P64 (Magdalen College) — Three fragments of Matthew 26; notable for early "Nomina Sacra."
  • P32 GA: P32 (Rylands Library) — An early fragment containing portions of the Epistle to Titus.

200 – 300 AD

  • P45 GA: P45 (Chester Beatty Library) — Fragments of all four Gospels and Acts.
  • P47 GA: P47 (Chester Beatty Library) — Portions of the Book of Revelation.
  • Oxyrhynchus Papyri GA: Various (Oxyrhynchus Online) — Numerous fragments including Matthew and various non-canonical works.
  • P1 GA: P1 (Univ. of Pennsylvania) — An early papyrus containing the beginning of Matthew 1.
  • P13 GA: P13 (British Library) — A significant scroll fragment containing large portions of Hebrews.
  • Dura-Europos GA: 0212 (Yale Beinecke Library) — A unique Greek fragment found in Syria, potentially an early Diatessaron harmony.
  • P37 GA: P37 (Univ. of Michigan) — A well-preserved fragment of Matthew 26 in a formal hand.

300 – 400 AD

  • Codex Sinaiticus GA: א (01) (British Library) — One of the most important complete manuscripts of the Bible.
  • Codex Vaticanus GA: B (03) (Vatican Library) — Major uncial manuscript containing most of the Old and New Testaments.
  • Codex Vercellensis GA: it-a (Biblioteca Capitolare) — The most important manuscript of the Old Latin version of the Gospels.
  • P87 GA: P87 (Univ. of Cologne) — The oldest known fragment of the Epistle to Phile
