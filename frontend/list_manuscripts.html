List of Manuscripts

Early Christian manuscripts dating from the first five centuries, organised chronologically in 100-year blocks. This list covers key papyri and major uncial codices.

0 – 100 AD

  • Fragmentary Evidence — While no complete New Testament manuscripts date to this first century, the oral and early written traditions that form the basis of the records are active.

100 – 200 AD

  • P52 (Rylands Library Papyrus P52) c. AD 125 — The oldest widely accepted fragment of the New Testament (John 18).
  • P66 (Papyrus Bodmer II) c. AD 200 — A near-complete codex of the Gospel of John.
  • P46 (Chester Beatty Papyrus II) c. AD 175-225 — The earliest collection of Pauline epistles.
  • P75 (Papyrus Bodmer XIV–XV) c. AD 175-225 — Extensive remains of Luke and John.

200 – 300 AD

  • P45 (Chester Beatty Papyrus I) c. AD 250 — Fragments of all four Gospels and Acts.
  • P47 (Chester Beatty Papyrus III) c. AD 250-300 — Portions of the Book of Revelation.
  • Oxyrhynchus Papyri 3rd Century — Numerous fragments including portions of Matthew and various non-canonical works.

300 – 400 AD

  • Codex Sinaiticus c. AD 330-360 — One of the most important complete manuscripts of the Bible.
  • Codex Vaticanus c. AD 325-350 — A world-class uncial manuscript containing most of the Old and New Testaments.

400 – 500 AD

  • Codex Alexandrinus 5th Century — A major uncial manuscript containing the Septuagint and the New Testament.
  • Codex Ephraemi Rescriptus 5th Century — A palimpsest containing much of the New Testament.
  • Codex Bezae c. AD 400 — A bilingual (Greek/Latin) manuscript of the Gospels and Acts.
