List of Manuscripts

Early Christian manuscripts dating from the first five centuries, organised chronologically in 100-year blocks. This list covers key fragements, papyri and major uncial codices.

33 – 200 AD

  • P52 c. AD 125 — Rylands Library Papyrus P52 — The oldest widely accepted fragment of the New Testament (John 18).
  • P66 c. AD 200 — Papyrus Bodmer II — A near-complete codex of the Gospel of John.
  • P46 c. AD 175-225 — Chester Beatty Papyrus II — The earliest collection of Pauline epistles.
  • P75 c. AD 175-225 — Papyrus Bodmer XIV–XV — Extensive remains of Luke and John.
  • P104 c. AD 150 — Oxyrhynchus Papyrus 4404 (Sackler Library) — An exceptionally early fragment of Matthew 21, contemporary with or slightly later than P52.
  • P90 c. AD 150 — Oxyrhynchus Papyrus 3523 (Sackler Library) — A small fragment of John 18, providing a second very early witness to the Fourth Gospel.
  • P64/P67 (Magdalen Papyrus) c. AD 175–200 — Magdalen College MS Gr. 18 — Three fragments of Matthew 26; notable for the early style of "Nomina Sacra."
  • P32 c. AD 200 — Rylands Library Papyrus P32

200 – 300 AD

  • P45 c. AD 250 — Chester Beatty Papyrus I — Fragments of all four Gospels and Acts.
  • P47 c. AD 250-300 — Chester Beatty Papyrus III — Portions of the Book of Revelation.
  • Oxyrhynchus Papyri 3rd Century — Oxyrhynchus Online Collections — Numerous fragments including portions of Matthew and various non-canonical works.
  • P1 c. AD 250 — University of Pennsylvania (E 2746) — An early papyrus; contains the beginning of Matthew 1.
  • P13 c. AD 250–300 — British Library Papyrus 1532 — A significant scroll fragment containing large portions of the Epistle to the Hebrews.
  • Dura-Europos Gospel Harmony (P.Dura 24) c. AD 235 — Yale University Beinecke Library — A unique Greek fragment found in Syria, potentially representing an early Diatessaron-style harmony.
  • P37 c. AD 250–300 — University of Michigan Papyrus 1570 — A well-preserved fragment of Matthew 26 written in a clear, formal hand.

300 – 400 AD

  • Codex Sinaiticus c. AD 330-360 — The Codex Sinaiticus Project (British Library) — One of the most important complete manuscripts of the Bible.
  • Codex Vaticanus c. AD 325-350 — Vatican Library (Vat. gr. 1209) — A world-class uncial manuscript containing most of the Old and New Testaments.
  • Codex Vercellensis c. AD 350 — Biblioteca Capitolare di Vercelli — The most important manuscript of the Old Latin (Vetus Latina) version of the Gospels.
  • P87 c. AD 300 — University of Cologne (Inv. theol. 12) — The oldest known fragment of the Epistle to Philemon.
  • Sinaitic Syriac (Codex Syriacus) 4th Century — St. Catherine's Monastery, Sinai — A palimpsest containing a 4th-century translation of the Gospels into Syriac, discovered underneath later text.
    • Codex Alexandrinus 5th Century — British Library (Royal MS 1 D VIII) — A major uncial manuscript containing the Septuagint and the New Testament.
    • Codex Ephraemi Rescriptus 5th Century — Bibliothèque nationale de France (Grec 9) — A palimpsest containing much of the New Testament.
    • Codex Bezae c. AD 400 — Cambridge University Library (MS Nn.2.41) — A bilingual (Greek/Latin) manuscript of the Gospels and Acts.
