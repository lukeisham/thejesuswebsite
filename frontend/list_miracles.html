List of Miracles

The miracles attributed to Jesus in the Gospel accounts, organised by category. Each entry includes the primary Gospel reference and a brief description.

Nature Miracles

  • Calming the Storm Mk 4:35–41 — Jesus rebukes the wind and waves on the Sea of Galilee.
  • Walking on Water Mt 14:22–33 — Jesus walks on the lake toward the disciples' boat at night.
  • Peter Walking on Water Matthew 14:28–31 — At Jesus' command, Peter briefly walks on the waves before faltering and being rescued.
  • Feeding the 5,000 Mk 6:30–44 — Five loaves and two fish multiply to feed a crowd of thousands.
  • Feeding the 4,000 Mk 8:1–10 — Seven loaves feed four thousand in a Gentile region.
  • Water into Wine Jn 2:1–11 — The first sign in John's Gospel, at the wedding in Cana.
  • Withering the Fig Tree Mk 11:12–25 — A cursed fig tree withers overnight, a sign of judgment.
  • Coin in the Fish's Mouth Mt 17:24–27 — A coin found in a fish's mouth pays the temple tax.
  • Miraculous Catch of Fish Lk 5:1–11; Jn 21:1–14 — The nets fill to breaking after a night of nothing.

Healing Miracles

  • Blind Bartimaeus Mk 10:46–52 — A blind beggar on the road to Jericho receives his sight.
  • Man Born Blind Jn 9:1–12 — Jesus heals a man blind from birth using mud and the Pool of Siloam.
  • Paralytic at Capernaum Mk 2:1–12 — Four friends lower a paralysed man through a roof to reach Jesus.
  • Woman with Bleeding Mk 5:25–34 — A woman healed after twelve years by touching Jesus' garment.
  • Ten Lepers Lk 17:11–19 — Ten are cleansed on their way to the priests; one Samaritan returns to give thanks.
  • Man with Withered Hand Mk 3:1–6 — Healed in a synagogue on the Sabbath, provoking the Pharisees.
  • Centurion's Servant Mt 8:5–13 — A Roman officer's faith secures healing from a distance.
  • Official's Son Jn 4:46–54 — A royal official's son is healed at the exact hour Jesus speaks.
  • Deaf-Mute Mk 7:31–37 — Jesus uses touch and the Aramaic word Ephphatha to open ears and loosen a tongue.
  • Malchus' Ear Lk 22:49–51 — The ear severed at Jesus' arrest is restored on the spot.
  • Simon’s Mother-in-Law Mark 1:29–31 — Jesus heals her of a high fever, and she immediately begins to serve them.
  • The Leper’s Cleansing Mark 1:40–45 — Jesus reaches out and touches an "unclean" man, instantly curing his leprosy.
  • Man at the Pool of Bethesda John 5:1–15 — An invalid for thirty-eight years is told to "pick up your mat and walk" on the Sabbath.
  • The Crippled Woman Luke 13:10–17 — Jesus heals a woman bent double for eighteen years, defending the act against synagogue leaders.
  • Man with Dropsy Luke 14:1–6 — Healed at a prominent Pharisee's house, sparking a debate about Sabbath laws.
  • The Multitudes at Evening Matthew 8:16–17, Mark 1:32–34 — After sunset, the whole town gathers at the door; Jesus heals all who are sick and casts out many spirits.
  • The Gennesaret Healings Matthew 14:34–36, Mark 6:53–56 — As Jesus passes through villages and marketplaces, people lay the sick in the streets, and all who touch the edge of his cloak are healed.
  • The Great Crowds by the Sea Matthew 15:29–31 — On a mountainside near the Sea of Galilee, the lame, blind, crippled, and mute are laid at his feet, and he heals them all.
  • The Judean Multitudes Matthew 19:1–2 — After leaving Galilee for the region of Judea, large crowds follow him, and he heals them there.

Exorcisms

  • Gerasene Demoniac Mk 5:1–20 — A man possessed by a legion of demons; spirits enter swine that rush into the sea.
  • Boy with Evil Spirit Mk 9:14–29 — Disciples fail where Jesus succeeds; "this kind only comes out by prayer."
  • Man in Capernaum Synagogue Mk 1:21–28 — The first exorcism in Mark, demonstrating authority over unclean spirits.
  • Mute Demoniac Mt 9:32–34 — A mute man speaks after the demon is cast out, dividing the crowd's reaction.
  • Syrophoenician Woman's Daughter Mk 7:24–30 — A Gentile woman's persistent faith secures deliverance at a distance.
  • The Blind and Mute Demoniac Matthew 12:22–23 — Jesus heals a possessed man who could neither see nor speak, leading the crowds to wonder if he is the Son of David.

Raising the Dead

  • Lazarus Jn 11:1–44 — Four days in the tomb; the centrepiece miracle of John's Gospel and a sign pointing to the resurrection.
  • Jairus' Daughter Mk 5:21–43 — A synagogue ruler's daughter raised with the Aramaic words Talitha Koum.
  • Widow of Nain's Son Lk 7:11–17 — Jesus stops a funeral procession and restores a widow's only son.
  • The Resurrection of the Saints Matthew 27:52–53 — At the moment of Jesus' death, tombs are opened and many holy people who had died are raised to life, appearing to many in Jerusalem after his resurrection.

Miracles of Knowledge

  • The Calling of Nathanael John 1:47–50 — Jesus demonstrates supernatural awareness of Nathanael’s location and interior character before their first physical meeting.
  • The Woman at the Well John 4:16–19 — Jesus reveals precise, intimate details of a Samaritan woman's domestic history, identifying her five previous husbands.
  • The Coin in the Fish's Mouth Matthew 17:24–27 — Jesus exhibits pre-cognitive knowledge, directing Peter to find a specific coin inside the first fish he catches.
  • Preparation for the Entry Mark 11:1–6 — Jesus provides detailed instructions about finding a specific colt tied in a village, including exactly what the owners would say.
  • The Upper Room Selection Luke 22:10–13 — Jesus predicts his disciples will meet a man carrying a water jar who will lead them to the specific location for the Last Supper.

Miracles of Survival

  • Escaping the Nazareth Mob Luke 4:28–30 — When his hometown neighbors attempt to execute him by throwing him off a cliff, Jesus supernaturally passes through the crowd to safety.
  • Passing Through the Temple Crowd John 8:59 — When the crowd in the Temple picks up stones to kill him, Jesus hides himself and supernaturally slips away from the grounds.
  • Evading Arrest in the Temple John 10:39 — After a confrontational discourse, the authorities attempt to seize him again, but he supernaturally escapes from their hands.
