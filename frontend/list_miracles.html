List of Miracles

The miracles attributed to Jesus in the Gospel accounts, organised by category. Each entry includes the primary Gospel reference and a brief description.

Nature Miracles

  • Calming the Storm Mk 4:35–41 — Jesus rebukes the wind and waves on the Sea of Galilee.
  • Walking on Water Mt 14:22–33 — Jesus walks on the lake toward the disciples' boat at night.
  • Feeding the 5,000 Mk 6:30–44 — Five loaves and two fish multiply to feed a crowd of thousands.
  • Feeding the 4,000 Mk 8:1–10 — Seven loaves feed four thousand in a Gentile region.
  • Water into Wine Jn 2:1–11 — The first sign in John's Gospel, at the wedding in Cana.
  • Withering the Fig Tree Mk 11:12–25 — A cursed fig tree withers overnight, a sign of judgment.
  • Coin in the Fish's Mouth Mt 17:24–27 — A coin found in a fish's mouth pays the temple tax.
  • Miraculous Catch of Fish Lk 5:1–11; Jn 21:1–14 — The nets fill to breaking after a night of nothing.

Healing Miracles

  • Blind Bartimaeus Mk 10:46–52 — A blind beggar on the road to Jericho receives his sight.
  • Man Born Blind Jn 9:1–12 — Jesus heals a man blind from birth using mud and the Pool of Siloam.
  • Paralytic at Capernaum Mk 2:1–12 — Four friends lower a paralysed man through a roof to reach Jesus.
  • Woman with Bleeding Mk 5:25–34 — A woman healed after twelve years by touching Jesus' garment.
  • Ten Lepers Lk 17:11–19 — Ten are cleansed on their way to the priests; one Samaritan returns to give thanks.
  • Man with Withered Hand Mk 3:1–6 — Healed in a synagogue on the Sabbath, provoking the Pharisees.
  • Centurion's Servant Mt 8:5–13 — A Roman officer's faith secures healing from a distance.
  • Official's Son Jn 4:46–54 — A royal official's son is healed at the exact hour Jesus speaks.
  • Deaf-Mute Mk 7:31–37 — Jesus uses touch and the Aramaic word Ephphatha to open ears and loosen a tongue.
  • Malchus' Ear Lk 22:49–51 — The ear severed at Jesus' arrest is restored on the spot.

Exorcisms

  • Gerasene Demoniac Mk 5:1–20 — A man possessed by a legion of demons; spirits enter swine that rush into the sea.
  • Boy with Evil Spirit Mk 9:14–29 — Disciples fail where Jesus succeeds; "this kind only comes out by prayer."
  • Man in Capernaum Synagogue Mk 1:21–28 — The first exorcism in Mark, demonstrating authority over unclean spirits.
  • Mute Demoniac Mt 9:32–34 — A mute man speaks after the demon is cast out, dividing the crowd's reaction.
  • Syrophoenician Woman's Daughter Mk 7:24–30 — A Gentile woman's persistent faith secures deliverance at a distance.

Raising the Dead

  • Lazarus Jn 11:1–44 — Four days in the tomb; the centrepiece miracle of John's Gospel and a sign pointing to the resurrection.
  • Jairus' Daughter Mk 5:21–43 — A synagogue ruler's daughter raised with the Aramaic words Talitha Koum.
  • Widow of Nain's Son Lk 7:11–17 — Jesus stops a funeral procession and restores a widow's only son.
