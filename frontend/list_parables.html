List of Parables

The parables of Jesus organised by theme — stories about the kingdom of God, grace, judgment, and the cost of discipleship.

Kingdom Parables

  • The Sower Mk 4:1–20 — Seed falls on four types of ground; only the good soil produces a harvest.
  • The Mustard Seed Mk 4:30–32 — The smallest seed becomes the largest garden plant — the kingdom begins small but grows vast.
  • The Leaven Mt 13:33 — A small amount of yeast works through the whole batch of dough.
  • Hidden Treasure Mt 13:44 — A man sells everything to buy a field containing hidden treasure.
  • Pearl of Great Price Mt 13:45–46 — A merchant sells all he has to purchase one supremely valuable pearl.
  • Wheat & Weeds Mt 13:24–30 — Good and evil grow together until the final harvest and separation.
  • The Dragnet Mt 13:47–50 — A net catches all kinds of fish; the good are kept, the bad thrown away at the end of the age.

Parables of Grace

  • The Prodigal Son Lk 15:11–32 — A son wastes his inheritance; the father runs to meet him on his return.
  • The Good Samaritan Lk 10:25–37 — A despised Samaritan shows mercy where religious leaders do not.
  • The Lost Sheep Lk 15:3–7 — A shepherd leaves ninety-nine to find the one that is lost.
  • The Lost Coin Lk 15:8–10 — A woman searches the whole house for one lost coin, then celebrates.
  • The Pharisee & Tax Collector Lk 18:9–14 — The humble prayer of the tax collector is heard; the Pharisee's is not.

Parables of Judgment

  • Rich Man & Lazarus Lk 16:19–31 — A beggar and a rich man face opposite fates after death.
  • Ten Virgins Mt 25:1–13 — Five wise virgins prepare; five foolish ones are shut out when the bridegroom arrives late.
  • The Talents Mt 25:14–30 — Servants are entrusted with different amounts; those who invest are rewarded, the hoarder is rebuked.
  • Sheep & Goats Mt 25:31–46 — At the final judgment, nations are separated based on how they treated the vulnerable.
  • Wicked Tenants Mk 12:1–12 — Tenants kill the owner's son; the vineyard is given to others.
  • Unmerciful Servant Mt 18:21–35 — A forgiven debtor refuses to forgive a smaller debt; faces severe consequences.

Parables of Discipleship

  • Wise & Foolish Builders Mt 7:24–27 — One builds on rock, one on sand; only the first survives the storm.
  • Counting the Cost Lk 14:28–33 — A builder and a king both plan before they commit — following Jesus requires full surrender.
  • Persistent Widow Lk 18:1–8 — A widow's persistence before an unjust judge illustrates persevering prayer.
  • Friend at Midnight Lk 11:5–8 — A man's bold persistence secures bread from a sleeping neighbour — an image of bold prayer.
