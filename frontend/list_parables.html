List of Parables

All the parables of Jesus organised into four themes: kingdom, grace, discipleship and judgement.

Kingdom Parables

  • The Sower Matthew 13:3-9, Mark 4:3-9, Luke 8:4-8 — Seed falls on four types of ground; only the good soil produces a harvest.
  • The Mustard Seed Matthew 13:31-32, Mark 4:30-32, Luke 13:18-19 — The smallest seed becomes the largest garden plant — the kingdom begins small but grows vast.
  • The Leaven Matthew 13:33, Luke 13:20-21 — A small amount of yeast works through the whole batch of dough.
  • Hidden Treasure Matthew 13:44 — A man sells everything to buy a field containing hidden treasure.
  • Pearl of Great Price Matthew 13:45-46 — A merchant sells all he has to purchase one supremely valuable pearl.
  • Growing Seed Mark 4:26-29 — The growth of God’s Kingdom is a mysterious, divine process that happens beyond human effort or understanding.
  • The Net Matthew 13:47–50 — A net catches all kinds of fish; the good are kept, the bad thrown away at the end of the age.
  • The Budding Fig Tree Matthew 24:32-35, Mark 13:28-31, Luke 21:29-33 — Signs in nature indicate that the Kingdom is near and the season is changing.

Parables of Grace

  • The Prodigal Son Luke 15:11-32 — A son wastes his inheritance; the father runs to meet him on his return.
  • The Good Samaritan Luke 10:25–37 — A despised Samaritan shows mercy where religious leaders do not.
  • The Lost Sheep Matthew 18:12-14, Luke 15:3-7 — A shepherd leaves ninety-nine to find the one that is lost.
  • The Lost Coin Luke 15:8-10 — A woman searches the whole house for one lost coin, then celebrates.
  • The Pharisee and Tax Collector Luke 18:9-14 — The humble prayer of the tax collector is heard; the Pharisee's is not.
  • Laborers in the Vineyard Matthew 20:1-16 — Workers hired at the eleventh hour receive the same wage as those hired at dawn — grace is not earned by hours worked.
  • The Two Debtors Luke 7:41-43 — A creditor forgives two debts of different sizes; the one forgiven more loves more.
  • The Great Banquet Luke 14:15-24 — When the invited guests make excuses, the host invites the outcasts and the poor to fill his house.

Parables of Discipleship

  • Wise and Foolish Builders Matthew 7:24-27, Luke 6:46-49 — One builds on rock, one on sand; only the first survives the storm.
  • Counting the Cost Luke 14:28-33 — A builder and a king both plan before they commit — following Jesus requires full surrender.
  • Persistent Widow Luke 18:1-8 — A widow's persistence before an unjust judge illustrates persevering prayer.
  • Friend at Midnight Luke 11:5–8 — A man's bold persistence secures bread from a sleeping neighbour — an image of bold prayer.
  • The Unjust Steward Luke 16:1-13 — A manager uses his master's resources to secure his own future — disciples are called to use worldly wealth for eternal ends.
  • The Two Sons Matthew 21:28-32 — One son says no but obeys; the other says yes but doesn't — discipleship is defined by action, not words.
  • Lamp Under a Bushel Matthew 5:14-16, Mark 4:21-25, Luke 8:16-18 — A lamp is meant to give light to all, just as a disciple’s faith should be visible to the world.
  • Salt of the Earth Matthew 5:13, Mark 9:50, Luke 14:34-35 — Disciples must retain their distinct character to be useful to the world.
  • The Watchful Servants Mark 13:34-37, Luke 12:35-40 — Servants must stay awake and ready for their master's return at any hour.
  • New Wine in Old Wineskins Matthew 9:17, Mark 2:22, Luke 5:37-38 — The new life of the Gospel cannot be contained within old, rigid legalistic structures.

Parables of Judgment

  • Rich Man and Lazarus Lk 16:19–31 — A beggar and a rich man face opposite fates after death.
  • Ten Virgins Matthew 25:1-13 — Five wise virgins prepare; five foolish ones are shut out when the bridegroom arrives late.
  • The Talents Matthew 25:14-30 — Servants are entrusted with different amounts; those who invest are rewarded, the hoarder is rebuked.
  • Sheep and Goats Matthew 25:31–46 — At the final judgment, nations are separated based on how they treated the vulnerable.
  • Wicked Tenants Matthew 21:33-46, Mark 12:1-12, Luke 20:9-19 — Tenants kill the owner's son; the vineyard is given to others.
  • Unmerciful Servant Matthew 18:21–35 — A forgiven debtor refuses to forgive a smaller debt; faces severe consequences.
  • Wheat and Weeds Matthew 13:24-30 — The righteous and unrighteous grow together until the final harvest and separation.
  • The Rich Fool Luke 12:16-21 — A man prioritizes earthly storage over his soul and loses everything in a single night.
  • Barren Fig Tree Luke 13:6-9 — A tree is given a limited time to bear fruit before it is cut down — a call for urgent repentance.
  • Wedding Garment Matthew 22:11-14 — A guest at a banquet is cast out for lacking the proper attire, representing the necessity of being "clothed" in righteousness.
  • The Blind Leading the Blind Matthew 15:14, Luke 6:39 — Spiritual leaders who lack vision will lead their followers into a pit.
  • The Tree and its Fruit Matthew 7:16-20, Luke 6:43-45 — A person's true nature and eventual judgment are revealed by the "fruit" of their life.
