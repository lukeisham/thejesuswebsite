The Jesus Website

An encyclopaedic survey of the historical evidence

This website is an comprehensive survey of the historical evidence for Jesus the Messiah. It brings together about 300 historical data points related to Jesus from the four gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke & John), and organises them by a visual sequence, a timeline, and then plots them on interactive maps of first century Galilee, Judea, Jerusalem and the wider Roman world. The plan is to slowly build out the context section with essays about various historical aspects to provide background and context for the core record of historical information

History is about reconstructing an accurate narrative of the past. Therefore the debate and discussion section catalogues the historiography of studying the historical Jesus and provides responses to popular and academic challenges to the historical Jesus. (There's a bonus section ranking all the Wikipedia articles related to Jesus from least reliable to most reliable.) Lists of all of Jesus' parables, key Old Testament prophecies, Jesus' sermons and sayings, etc are in the resources section. And you can find out more about the project in the about section.
