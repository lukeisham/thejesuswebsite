List of Key Events

The major events in the life of Jesus as recorded in the Gospel accounts, arranged chronologically by phase.

Birth to Ministry

  • Annunciation Luke 1:26–38 — The angel Gabriel announces to Mary that she will conceive the Son of God.
  • Joseph's First Dream Matthew 1:18–25 — An angel appears to Joseph in a dream, instructing him to take Mary as his wife and name the child Jesus.
  • The Visitation Luke 1:39–56 — Mary visits her relative Elizabeth; both celebrate the miraculous nature of their pregnancies.
  • The Journey to Bethlehem Luke 2:1–5 — Due to the census ordered by Caesar Augustus, Joseph travels with the pregnant Mary from Nazareth to his ancestral home in Judea.
  • Nativity Luke 2:1–7 — Jesus is born in Bethlehem and laid in a manger because there was no room in the guest quarters.
  • Announcement to the Shepherds Luke 2:8–14 — An angel and a heavenly host appear to shepherds in the fields, proclaiming the birth of the Savior.
  • The Shepherds' Visit Luke 2:15–20 — Following the angelic sign, the shepherds hurry to Bethlehem to find the child and then spread the news of what they had seen.
  • Circumcision & Naming Luke 2:21 — On the eighth day, the child is circumcised according to the Law and officially given the name Jesus.
  • Presentation at the Temple Luke 2:22–40 — Simeon and Anna recognise the infant Jesus as the Messiah during his dedication at forty days old.
  • Visit of the Magi Matthew 2:1–12 — Wise men from the East follow a star and bring gifts to the infant Jesus.
  • Massacre of the Innocents Matthew 2:16–18 — King Herod orders the execution of young male children in Bethlehem to eliminate the "King of the Jews."
  • Flight to Egypt Matthew 2:13–23 — The family flees the massacre and remains in Egypt until the death of Herod.
  • Finding in the Temple Luke 2:41–52 — At age twelve, Jesus stays behind in Jerusalem to engage with the teachers in the Temple.
  • The Years in Nazareth Luke 2:51–52 — Jesus returns to Nazareth and remains in obedience to his parents, growing in wisdom and stature.

Preparation

  • Baptism by John Mark 1:9–11 — Jesus is baptised in the Jordan; the Spirit descends and the Father speaks.
  • Temptation in the Wilderness Matthew 4:1–11 — Forty days of fasting; Satan tests Jesus with bread, power, and glory.

Ministry Events

  • Call of the First Disciples Mark 1:16–20 — Jesus calls Simon, Andrew, James, and John by the Sea of Galilee to become "fishers of men."
  • Rejection at Nazareth Luke 4:16–30 — Jesus reads from Isaiah in his hometown synagogue; the crowd attempts to throw him off a cliff after he claims fulfillment of the prophecy.
  • Calling of the Twelve Mark 3:13–19 — Jesus officially appoints twelve apostles from among his larger following to be his primary emissaries.
  • The Sending of the Seventy Luke 10:1–20 — Jesus commissions a large group of followers to go ahead of him to every town he intends to visit.
  • Peter's Confession Matthew 16:13–20 — At Caesarea Philippi, Peter declares Jesus to be "the Christ, the Son of the living God."
  • Transfiguration Mark 9:2–13 — Jesus' appearance is transformed on a mountain; Moses and Elijah appear to discuss his coming departure.
  • Visit to Mary and Martha Luke 10:38–42 — A narrative moment in Bethany where Jesus teaches on the "one thing necessary" while being hosted.
  • The Anointing at Bethany John 12:1–8 — Mary of Bethany anoints Jesus with costly nard, an act Jesus interprets as preparation for his burial.
  • Triumphal Entry Mark 11:1–11 — Jesus enters Jerusalem on a donkey, fulfilling Zechariah’s prophecy while the crowds proclaim him King.
  • Cleansing the Temple Mark 11:15–19 — Jesus disruptively drives the merchants out of the Temple courts, declaring it a "house of prayer for all nations."

Passion & Death

  • Washing the Disciples' Feet John 13:1–17 — Jesus performs a servant's task to demonstrate the leadership model of his Kingdom.
  • Last Supper Mark 14:12–26 — Jesus shares a final Passover meal and identifies the bread and wine as his body and blood.
  • Agony in Gethsemane Mark 14:32–42 — Jesus prays in anguish while his disciples sleep; he surrenders his life to the Father's will.
  • The Sleeping Disciples Mark 14:37–41, Matthew 26:40–45 — Jesus returns from his prayer in Gethsemane three times to find Peter, James, and John asleep, unable to keep watch with him in his hour of distress.
  • Arrest Mark 14:43–52 — Betrayed by Judas with a kiss; Jesus is seized by the temple guard in the garden.
  • Interrogation by Annas John 18:12–24 — Before seeing Caiaphas, Jesus is first questioned by the former High Priest regarding his disciples and teaching.
  • Trial before the Sanhedrin Matthew 26:57–68 — The high priest Caiaphas and the council find Jesus guilty of blasphemy after he identifies himself as the Son of Man.
  • Peter's Denial Luke 22:54–62 — While Jesus is under interrogation, Peter three times denies knowing him before the rooster crows.
  • The Remorse of Judas Matthew 27:3–10 — Overwhelmed by guilt, Judas returns the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests before taking his own life.
  • The Handover to Pilate Matthew 27:1–2, Mark 15:1 — Early in the morning, the religious leaders bind Jesus and lead him away to hand him over to the Roman jurisdiction of Pontius Pilate.
  • Trial before Pilate Mark 15:1–15 — The Roman governor finds no guilt but yields to the crowd; sentences Jesus to crucifixion.
  • The Dream of Pilate's Wife Matthew 27:19 — Pilate's wife sends an urgent message to "have nothing to do with that innocent man" because of a dream she suffered.
  • The Jurisdiction Referral Luke 23:6–7 — Discovering Jesus is from Galilee, Pilate attempts to avoid responsibility by sending him to Herod Antipas.
  • The Mockery by Herod Luke 23:8–11 — Herod hopes for a miracle; when met with silence, he and his soldiers mock Jesus in a royal robe and send him back.
  • The Friendship of Enemies Luke 23:12 — Pilate and Herod, who had previously been at odds, become friends as a result of their shared involvement in the trial.
  • The Release of Barabbas Mark 15:6–15 — Given a choice by Pilate, the crowd demands the release of a known insurrectionist instead of Jesus.
  • The Washing of Hands Matthew 27:24–25 — Pilate washes his hands in front of the crowd, declaring himself innocent of Jesus' blood.
  • Scourging and Mockery John 19:1–3 — Roman soldiers flog Jesus and place a crown of thorns on his head, hailing him as "King of the Jews."
  • Ecce Homo ("Behold the Man") John 19:4–7 — Pilate presents the beaten Jesus to the crowd in a failed attempt to elicit pity and release him.
  • Bearing the Cross John 19:16–17 — After final sentencing, Jesus is led away, carrying his own cross toward the place of execution.
  • The Way of Sorrows (Via Dolorosa) Luke 23:26–31 — Simon of Cyrene is forced to help carry the cross, and Jesus speaks to the "Daughters of Jerusalem."
  • Crucifixion Mark 15:21–41 — Jesus is crucified at Golgotha; he suffers and dies while soldiers gamble for his clothing.
  • The Two Criminals Luke 23:39–43 — While one criminal hurls insults, the other recognizes Jesus' innocence and is promised a place in Paradise.
  • The Tearing of the Veil Matthew 27:51, Mark 15:38 — At the moment of Jesus' death, the heavy curtain of the Temple is torn in two from top to bottom, signifying the opening of the way to God.
  • The Centurion's Testimony Matthew 27:54, Mark 15:39 — Seeing the earthquake and the manner in which Jesus died, the Roman centurion in charge of the execution declares, "Truly this man was the Son of God."
  • The Division of Garments Matthew 27:35–36 — While Jesus hangs on the cross, soldiers cast lots for his clothing, fulfilling Messianic prophecy.
  • Burial Mark 15:42–47 — Joseph of Arimathea places Jesus in a new tomb and seals it with a large stone.
  • The Request for a Guard Matthew 27:62–65 — The chief priests and Pharisees petition Pilate to secure the tomb, fearing the disciples might steal the body; Pilate grants them a guard of Roman soldiers.
  • The Sealing of the Tomb Matthew 27:66 — The religious authorities go to the tomb, make it secure by putting a seal on the stone, and station the guard to prevent any interference.

Resurrection & Appearances

  • The Earthquake & Angel Matthew 28:2–4 — A violent earthquake occurs as an angel of the Lord rolls back the stone and sits on it, paralyzing the guards with fear.
  • The Empty Tomb Luke 24:1–12 — On the first day of the week, the women find the stone rolled away and the body gone; they depart in fear and joy.
  • The Race to the Tomb John 20:3–10 — After hearing Mary Magdalene's report, Peter and the "Beloved Disciple" run to the tomb; John arrives first, but Peter is the first to enter.
  • The Resurrection Matthew 28:5–7 — The angel announces the miraculous news to the women: "He is not here; he has risen, just as he said."
  • The Commissioning of the Women Matthew 28:7–8, Mark 16:7 — The angel instructs the women to go quickly and tell the disciples that Jesus is going ahead of them into Galilee.
  • The Second Appearance to the Women Matthew 28:9–10 — As they run to tell the disciples, Jesus meets them; they take hold of his feet and worship him as he repeats the command to tell his brothers.
  • Appearance to Mary Magdalene John 20:11–18 — Jesus meets Mary in the garden; she initially mistakes him for the gardener until he calls her by name.
  • The Report to the Disciples Luke 24:9–11 — The women return from the tomb and report everything to the Eleven and the others, but their words seem like "idle tales" and are not believed.
  • The Bribing of the Guards Matthew 28:11–15 — The guards report the events to the chief priests, who pay them to spread the false rumor that the disciples stole the body.
  • Road to Emmaus Luke 24:13–35 — The risen Jesus walks with two disciples, explaining the scriptures before being recognized in the breaking of bread.
  • Appearance to the Eleven John 20:19–23 — Jesus appears in a locked room, breathes on the disciples, and grants them the Holy Spirit.
  • Appearance to the Others Luke 24:33–43 — Jesus appears to those gathered with the Eleven; he proves his physical resurrection by showing his wounds and eating broiled fish.
  • Appearance to the Many Matthew 28:16–17 — Jesus appears to the larger body of disciples on a mountain in Galilee, where some worshiped him but others still struggled with doubt.
  • Appearance to Thomas John 20:24–29 — One week later, Jesus invites Thomas to touch his wounds, leading to Thomas' confession: "My Lord and my God!"
  • Breakfast on the Beach John 21:1–14 — Jesus appears to seven disciples by the Sea of Galilee, providing a miraculous catch of fish and sharing a meal.
  • Restoration of Peter John 21:15–19 — By the charcoal fire, Jesus asks Peter three times if he loves him, commissioning him to "Feed my sheep."
  • The Great Commission Matthew 28:16–20 — On a mountain in Galilee, Jesus commands his followers to go and make disciples of all nations.
  • The Ascension Luke 24:50–53 — After blessing his disciples at Bethany, Jesus is taken up into heaven while they worship him.

Historical & Political Context

  • The Decree of Caesar Augustus Luke 2:1 — The Roman Emperor issues a decree for a world-wide census, providing the catalyst for the journey to Bethlehem.
  • The Governorship of Quirinius Luke 2:2 — A chronological marker identifying the Roman administration in Syria during the time of the census.
  • The Reign of Tiberius Caesar Luke 3:1–2 — A detailed historical anchor marking the fifteenth year of the Emperor's reign and identifying the various regional tetrarchs.
  • The Marriage of Herod and Herodias Mark 6:17–18 — The controversial and unlawful marriage between Herod Antipas and his brother's wife, which created political and religious scandal.
  • The Slaughter of the Galileans Luke 13:1 — A contemporary tragedy where Pontius Pilate executed Galilean pilgrims, mingling their blood with their sacrifices.
  • The Collapse of the Tower in Siloam Luke 13:4 — A localized disaster in Jerusalem where a tower fell and killed eighteen people.

Narrative of John the Baptist

  • Annunciation to Zechariah Luke 1:5–25 — The angel Gabriel appears in the Temple to announce that the elderly Zechariah and Elizabeth will have a son.
  • The Birth & Naming of John Luke 1:57–66 — Elizabeth gives birth; Zechariah regains his speech only after confirming the name "John" on a writing tablet.
  • John’s Wilderness Preparation Luke 1:80 — A summary of John's early life, growing in spirit and living in the desert until his public appearance.
  • The Imprisonment of John Luke 3:19–20 — Herod Antipas locks John in prison because of his rebuke regarding Herodias.
  • The Birthday Feast of Herod Mark 6:21–23 — A grand banquet for Galillean nobles where the daughter of Herodias performs a dance that pleases the king.
  • The Execution of John the Baptist Mark 6:24–29 — At the request of Herodias, John is beheaded in prison; his head is brought on a platter and his disciples later bury his body.
