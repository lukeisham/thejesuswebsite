List of Key Events

The major events in the life of Jesus as recorded in the Gospel accounts, arranged chronologically by phase.

Birth & Infancy

  • Annunciation Lk 1:26–38 — The angel Gabriel announces to Mary that she will conceive the Son of God.
  • Nativity Lk 2:1–20 — Jesus is born in Bethlehem during a Roman census; announced to shepherds by angels.
  • Visit of the Magi Mt 2:1–12 — Wise men from the East follow a star and bring gifts to the infant Jesus.
  • Presentation at the Temple Lk 2:22–40 — Simeon and Anna recognise the infant Jesus as the Messiah.
  • Flight to Egypt Mt 2:13–23 — The family flees Herod's massacre of the innocents and returns after his death.

Preparation

  • Baptism by John Mk 1:9–11 — Jesus is baptised in the Jordan; the Spirit descends and the Father speaks.
  • Temptation in the Wilderness Mt 4:1–11 — Forty days of fasting; Satan tests Jesus with bread, power, and glory.

Ministry

  • Calling of the Twelve Mk 3:13–19 — Jesus appoints twelve apostles from among his followers.
  • Sermon on the Mount Mt 5–7 — The foundational ethical and kingdom teaching of Jesus, including the Beatitudes.
  • Transfiguration Mk 9:2–13 — Jesus' appearance changes on a mountain; Moses and Elijah appear; the Father speaks.
  • Triumphal Entry Mk 11:1–11 — Jesus enters Jerusalem on a donkey, welcomed with palm branches and shouts of Hosanna.
  • Cleansing the Temple Mk 11:15–19 — Jesus overturns the tables of money-changers in the Temple courts.

Passion & Death

  • Last Supper Mk 14:12–26 — Jesus shares a Passover meal and institutes the Eucharist with his disciples.
  • Agony in Gethsemane Mk 14:32–42 — Jesus prays in anguish while disciples sleep; surrenders to the Father's will.
  • Arrest Mk 14:43–52 — Betrayed by Judas with a kiss; seized by the temple guard in the garden.
  • Trial before Pilate Mk 15:1–15 — Roman governor finds no guilt but yields to the crowd; sentences Jesus to crucifixion.
  • Crucifixion Mk 15:21–41 — Jesus is crucified at Golgotha; darkness falls; he dies crying out to God.
  • Burial Mk 15:42–47 — Joseph of Arimathea places Jesus in a new tomb; a stone is rolled over the entrance.

Resurrection & Beyond

  • Empty Tomb Mk 16:1–8 — Women find the stone rolled away and an angel announcing Jesus has risen.
  • Appearances 1 Cor 15:3–8 — Jesus appears to Mary, the Twelve, 500 disciples, James, and Paul.
  • Ascension Acts 1:9–11 — Jesus is taken up into heaven forty days after the resurrection.
  • Pentecost Acts 2:1–41 — The Holy Spirit descends on the disciples; 3,000 believe and are baptised.
