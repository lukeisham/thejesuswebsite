Sayings & Sermons

The major discourses and key sayings of Jesus, organised by category — from the Sermon on the Mount to the "I Am" statements in John's Gospel.

Major Sermons & Discourses

  • Sermon on the Mount Matthew 5:1–7:29 — The Beatitudes, the Lord's Prayer, and ethical teaching on anger, lust, oaths, prayer, and wealth.
  • Sermon on the Plain Luke 6:17–49 — Luke's parallel account focusing on blessings and woes, love of enemies, and the command not to judge.
  • Olivet Discourse Matthew 24:1–25:46, Mark 13:1–37, Luke 21:5–36 — Teachings on the destruction of Jerusalem, signs of the end times, and the coming of the Son of Man.
  • Farewell Discourse John 14:1–17:26 — Jesus prepares the disciples for his departure, promises the Holy Spirit, and offers the High Priestly Prayer for unity.
  • Bread of Life Discourse John 6:22–71 — Following the feeding of the five thousand, Jesus identifies himself as the true bread from heaven.

"I Am" Sayings (John)

  • Bread of Life John 6:35–48 — "I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me will never hunger, and whoever believes in me will never be thirsty."
  • Light of the World John 8:12, 9:5 — "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness but will have the light of life."
  • The Gate John 10:7–9 — "I am the gate for the sheep; whoever enters through me will be saved and will find pasture."
  • The Good Shepherd John 10:11–14 — "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep."
  • The Resurrection & the Life John 11:25 — "I am the resurrection and the life. The one who believes in me will live, even though they die."
  • The Way, the Truth & the Life John 14:6 — "I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me."
  • The True Vine John 15:1–5 — "I am the true vine, and my Father is the gardener. Remain in me, as I also remain in you."

Kingdom Sayings

  • "The kingdom of God is at hand" Matthew 4:17, Mark 1:15 — The opening proclamation of Jesus' public ministry calling for repentance and belief.
  • "Unless you become like children" Matthew 18:3, Mark 10:14–15 — The essential posture of humility and dependence required to enter the kingdom of God.
  • "Seek first the kingdom" Matthew 6:33, Luke 12:31 — The command to prioritize God’s reign above material anxiety, clothing, and provision.
  • "The kingdom of God is within you" Luke 17:20–21 — Jesus' response to Pharisees, indicating the kingdom's present, internal, or "among you" reality.

Ethical Sayings

  • "Love your enemies" Matthew 5:43–48, Luke 6:27–36 — The call to pray for persecutors and demonstrate love that transcends human reciprocity.
  • "Do not judge" Matthew 7:1–5, Luke 6:37–42 — A warning that the measure of judgment we apply to others will be applied to us by God.
  • The Golden Rule Matthew 7:12, Luke 6:31 — "So in everything, do to others what you would have them do to you, for this sums up the Law and the Prophets."
  • "Render unto Caesar" Matthew 22:15–22, Mark 12:13–17, Luke 20:20–26 — A brilliant distinction between civic responsibility to the state and total loyalty to God.
  • "The Greatest Commandment" Matthew 22:34–40, Mark 12:28–34 — Love God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength; love your neighbor as yourself.

Commission & Mission

  • Missionary Discourse Matthew 10:1–42 — Instructions to the twelve on preaching, healing, and enduring the persecution that follows the Gospel.
  • Community Discourse Matthew 18:1–35 — Teachings on humility, seeking the lost, and the necessity of radical forgiveness among believers.
  • The Great Commission Matthew 28:18–20 — The final command to go into all the world, baptizing and teaching all nations.
  • The Woes to the Pharisees Matthew 23:1–39 — A sharp critique of hypocrisy, legalism, and the neglect of justice and mercy.
  • "Take Up Your Cross" Matthew 16:24, Mark 8:34, Luke 9:23 — The call to self-denial and total commitment as the prerequisite for following Jesus.

Jesus' Seven Last Words from the Cross

  • A Prayer for Forgiveness Luke 23:34 — "Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing."
  • The Promise to the Thief Luke 23:43 — "Truly I tell you, today you will be with me in paradise."
  • Provision for His Mother John 19:26–27 — "Woman, here is your son... Here is your mother" — Jesus entrusts Mary to the care of the beloved disciple.
  • The Cry of Dereliction Matthew 27:46, Mark 15:34 — "Eloi, Eloi, lema sabachthani?" (My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?).
  • The Cry of Physical Need John 19:28 — "I am thirsty" — fulfilling the Scripture that the Messiah would be given vinegar to drink.
  • The Cry of Triumph John 19:30 — "It is finished" — announcing the completion of his redemptive work on earth.
  • The Final Commendation Luke 23:46 — "Father, into your hands I commit my spirit" — Jesus' final breath as he voluntarily gives up his life.
