Sayings & Sermons

The major discourses and key sayings of Jesus, organised by category — from the Sermon on the Mount to the "I Am" statements in John's Gospel.

Major Sermons & Discourses

  • Sermon on the Mount Mt 5–7 — The Beatitudes, the Lord's Prayer, ethical teaching on anger, lust, oaths, prayer and wealth.
  • Sermon on the Plain Lk 6:17–49 — Luke's parallel account with blessings and woes; love of enemies; judging others.
  • Olivet Discourse Mk 13 — Teaching on the destruction of Jerusalem, signs of the end, and the coming of the Son of Man.
  • Farewell Discourse Jn 14–17 — Jesus prepares the disciples for his departure; promises the Holy Spirit; prays for unity.
  • Bread of Life Discourse Jn 6:22–71 — Jesus identifies himself as the bread from heaven; many disciples turn back.

"I Am" Sayings (John)

  • Bread of Life Jn 6:35 — "I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me will never hunger."
  • Light of the World Jn 8:12 — "Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness but will have the light of life."
  • The Gate Jn 10:9 — "I am the gate; whoever enters through me will be saved."
  • The Good Shepherd Jn 10:11 — "The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep."
  • The Resurrection & the Life Jn 11:25 — "Whoever believes in me, even though they die, will live."
  • The Way, the Truth & the Life Jn 14:6 — "No one comes to the Father except through me."
  • The True Vine Jn 15:1 — "Remain in me, as I also remain in you. No branch can bear fruit by itself."

Kingdom Sayings

  • "The kingdom of God is at hand" Mk 1:15 — The opening proclamation of Jesus' public ministry.
  • "Unless you become like children" Mt 18:3 — The posture required to enter the kingdom of God.
  • "Seek first the kingdom" Mt 6:33 — Prioritising the kingdom above material anxiety and provision.
  • "The kingdom of God is within you" Lk 17:21 — Jesus' response to Pharisees asking when the kingdom would come.

Ethical Sayings

  • "Love your enemies" Mt 5:44 — Pray for those who persecute you; love beyond reciprocity.
  • "Do not judge" Mt 7:1 — You will be judged by the same measure you apply to others.
  • The Golden Rule Mt 7:12 — "Do to others what you would have them do to you."
  • "Render unto Caesar" Mk 12:17 — The distinction between civil obligation and loyalty to God.
  • "The Greatest Commandment" Mk 12:29–31 — Love God with all your heart; love your neighbour as yourself.
