Arbor Diagram

Explore the relationships between historical evidence. The diagram shows how different pieces of evidence support, relate to, or lead to one another in the historical record.

How to Use This Diagram

  • Click any node to navigate to the full evidence detail page
  • Hover over a node to see more details in a tooltip
  • Drag to pan the diagram around the canvas
  • Use the zoom controls at the bottom to focus on specific areas
  • Node colors and styles indicate the type of relationship (see legend below)

Zoom: 100%

Node Types & Relationships

Root Evidence

Central or primary evidence from which other evidence branches

Supporting / Related

Evidence that supports or leads to the root evidence or other nodes

Tangentially Related

Evidence with loose or indirect relationships to the main narrative

About This Visualization

The Arbor Diagram provides a visual representation of how different pieces of historical evidence connect and relate to one another. The position and layout of nodes reflect their relationships in the historical record. Root nodes represent central or pivotal evidence, while branching nodes show supporting, derived, or related evidence. This structure helps researchers understand the interconnected nature of historical testimony about Jesus and the early Christian movement.
